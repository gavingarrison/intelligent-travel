# Halo Experience Framework

## Purpose

The Halo Experience framework is the company's structured evaluation system for deciding whether an experience deserves recommendation.

It should make taste more consistent without pretending that taste is purely mechanical.

## Core Question

Is this actually worth someone's limited time?

## Evaluation Dimensions

### 1. Memory Value

How likely is the experience to become a meaningful memory?

Signals:

- Distinctiveness
- Emotional resonance
- Family recall potential
- Sense of place
- Beauty
- Surprise

### 2. Taste And Quality

How strong is the underlying aesthetic, service, product, or cultural quality?

Signals:

- Design
- Craft
- Food or drink quality
- Hospitality
- Atmosphere
- Detail
- Editorial distinctiveness

### 3. Context Fit

For whom and under what circumstances is this experience actually good?

Signals:

- Family type
- Child age
- Occasion
- Season
- Weather
- Time of day
- Energy level
- Trip purpose

### 4. Time Value

Does the experience justify the time it requires?

Signals:

- Travel time
- Wait time
- Duration
- Planning complexity
- Opportunity cost
- Density of reward

### 5. Friction

What makes the experience harder than it looks?

Signals:

- Parking
- Crowds
- Reservations
- Service inconsistency
- Cost surprises
- Accessibility issues
- Kid-specific constraints

### 6. Trust Confidence

How confident are we in the recommendation?

Signals:

- Firsthand experience
- Recency
- Repeat visits
- Corroborating sources
- Reviewer expertise
- Consistency over time
- Known caveats

## Verdict Types

Recommended verdicts:

- Essential
- Strongly recommended
- Recommended with caveats
- Situational
- Not recommended
- Retired

## Scoring Guidance

Scores should support judgment, not replace it.

A useful evaluation should include:

- Dimension-level scores
- Written rationale
- Best-fit context
- Caveats
- Confidence level
- Last verification date

## Methodology Versioning

The framework should be versioned. If the methodology changes, old reviews should preserve the framework version used at the time of evaluation.
